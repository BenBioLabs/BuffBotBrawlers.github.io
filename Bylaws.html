Bylaws of BuffBot Brawlers

(as adapted from the BuffBot Brawlers Constitution)

https://www.colorado.edu/involvement/bylaws

ARTICLE I – Name of the Club

1.1: The name of this club shall be BuffBot Brawlers

ARTICLE II – Purpose of the Club

In the BuffBot Brawlers club, we want to foster an environment where people can learn, get hands-on experience, and apply hard and soft engineering skills. We will guide members in the process of designing and putting together their own 1lb - 3lb combat robots and compete with them in our open community tournaments, as well as abroad. This will engage basic engineering principles such as CAD, 3D printing, electrical engineering, fabrication, cooperation, and interdisciplinary communication. Regular tournaments will be open to everyone at the school and the wider Boulder/Denver community, outreach is important and we want to inspire and engage others in engineering like we were.

ARTICLE III – Officers of the Club

3.1: President

        The club president will be in charge of the overall management of the club organization. They will serve as an effective branch between the club and the University, serving to develop a positive partnership between the two entities. Further, they will work with nearby groups to prepare tournaments and competitive events. This individual will also serve to develop the options for the team’s future including working towards inclusion in events such as BattleBots and the NHRL Championships as the club develops.

3.2: Vice President

        The vice president assists the President with the above management as well as being the primary point of contact and leader for the development of combat robotic systems. They will help guide the club through major projects while keeping track of deadlines and project requirements.

3.3: Treasurer

The Treasurer of the club is responsible for the development of partnerships with external institutions to bring them in as sponsors of the club. These communications will directly benefit the club’s overall financial standing as well as the good standing of those aforementioned sponsors within the community. They will create sponsorship and financial programs for the club while ensuring the sponsors are justly compensated for their contributions.

3.4: Communications Officer

Manage internal and external communications of the club. Internal: Keep members updated on meetings and announcements, and make sure members and officers are communicating with each other. External: make sure people outside the club can see updates and information.

3.5: Logistics Officer

        The logistics role will be a part-time role possibly integrated with one or more of the other aforementioned roles. Their primary responsibility will be planning for travel and shipping to various events and contests, securing reservations for space within the University for meetings, construction, or events, and developing competition infrastructure such as the Robot Combat Arena.

3.6: Team Leads:

Each subteam of the development process will be led by so-called “experts” in various fields of robotics development. Following the traditional nomination process, the team leads are selected by a majority vote of the remaining officer team (President, Vice President, Treasurer, Communications Officer, Logistics Officer). These individuals will be responsible for assisting with and helping to develop the most difficult systems. These experts will be divided into five categories: Mechanical, Electrical, Programming, Integration, and CAD (Computer Aided Design).

3.7: Terms

The Term of office for all elected club members and officials shall be one school year with elections taking place every fall in either late August or Early September.

3.8: Elections

        The election process will be comprised of nominations wherein any individual receiving two or more nominations for the role may enter the voting process at which point all active members will receive one vote for each role. In case of a tie, all non-tied members will be dropped from the ballot and a revote cast. The President will not vote at any point and will have a deciding say in case this election results in a second tie.

3.9: Resignation

        Should an elected official choose to leave their post at some point throughout their term, an immediate “Emergency Election” shall take place wherein nominations are cast, and at the next regular meeting with a majority of active club members present, an election shall be held according to the above rules. The new candidate will serve until the end of their predecessor’s original term.

3.10: Impeachment

        An officer of the club may be impeached by a ⅔ majority vote of the other officers and team leads. An impeachment must be brought about by a club officer or team lead and seconded by another individual of the leadership team. Should an individual hold both an Expert and overall lead post, they will receive only one vote towards the procedure.

ARTICLE IV: Membership

4.1: General Statement of Eligibility for Membership

This club is open to any students in good standing of the University of Colorado Boulder.

4.2: Becoming a Member

        Membership in the club will be granted to any individuals who attend a minimum of five meetings within the first semester period. Membership also requires the signing of all club documents (waiver, etc…).

4.3: Good Standing

        To remain in good standing you must abide by all club bylaws. You must cast a vote in the yearly election (even if it is an abstain vote). Additionally, you must sign all club documents within a month of joining.

4.4: Recovering Good Standing        

If you fall out of good standing you may return to good standing by following the rules stated within Section 4.2 and 4.3. The President shall dictate whether sufficient action has been taken to warrant transition back to “Good Standing.”

ARTICLE V: Meetings and Parliamentary Operation

5.1: Meetings

Meetings will be held twice a week, for a minimum of one hour each. The dates will be determined by the availability of members, however, by default meetings will be 6:30-7:30 PM Tuesday and Friday in Williams Village North. There may be additional meetings depending on an as-needed basis. It is tradition to end meetings by watching an episode of BattleBots.

5.2: Parliamentary Authority

The rules contained in the current edition of Robert’s Rules of Order, Newly Revised, shall govern the Club in all cases to which they apply and in which they are not

inconsistent with the Constitution/Bylaws of the Club.

ARTICLE VI – Amendments

6.1: Amendment Proposition

The proposed amendment must be made available to club officers no later than one week before the scheduled meeting of the Club, at which it is to be presented to all members.

6.2: Voting Procedure

 The amendment shall pass by a 51% vote of the membership at the first meeting of the club at which a quorum is present. The President reserves the power to veto any amendments made (but not retroactively).

ARTICLE VIII – Enacting Clause

This constitution shall become effective following approval of the President and Vice President
